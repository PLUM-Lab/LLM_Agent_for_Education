# Medical Quiz – How to Use

This guide explains how to use the Medical Quiz app for practicing medical questions and getting help from the AI Tutor.

---

## Getting Started

### 1. Sign In

1. Enter your **username** and **password**
2. Click **Sign in**

**First time?** Click **Create account** to register, then sign in.

*Demo accounts: admin / admin or student / student*

### 2. Understand the Screen

After signing in, the page has two main areas:

**Left panel (top to bottom):**
- **Header:** "Logged in as: [username]", **Sign out** link, **Questions Answered**, **Knowledge Coverage** (e.g. 0% (0/193 subtopics))
- **By domain (accuracy %):** A list of knowledge domains with your accuracy for each (e.g. "Abdominal Wall & Hernias 0% (0/24)")
- **Question Bank:** Dropdown to select a question set (e.g. Surgery Domain Questions)
- **Question:** The current question with Domain, Topic, and Subtopic info
- **Choices:** Answer options (A, B, C, D, etc.)
- **Buttons:** Submit, Previous, Next, Jump to, Jump
- **Feedback:** Shows correct/wrong and explanations after you submit

**Right panel – Tutor:**
- AI Tutor that invites you to explain your reasoning
- Input box and **Send** button

You can drag the divider between the left and right panels to resize them.

---

## Practicing Questions

### Step 1: Choose a Question Bank

Use the **Question Bank** dropdown to pick a set of questions (e.g. Surgery Domain Questions). The app loads the questions and shows how many are available (e.g. "Loaded 579 questions").

### Step 2: Read and Answer

1. Read the question (and any images)
2. Click one answer option (A, B, C, D, etc.)
3. Click **Submit**

### Step 3: View Feedback

After submitting, the **Feedback** area shows:

- Whether your answer was **correct** or **wrong**
- Explanations for each option
- Where the content comes from (source references)

### Navigating Between Questions

- **Previous** – Go to the previous question
- **Next** – Go to the next question
- **Jump to** – Enter a question number and click **Jump** (or press Enter) to go straight to that question

---

## Using the Tutor

The right panel is your AI Tutor. When you open a question, it shows: *"I'd like to understand your thinking about this problem. Can you explain your reasoning? What are you considering?"*

### 1. Explain Your Reasoning (Type + Send)

1. Type your reasoning in the input box (e.g. what you considered, what confused you)
2. Click **Send** (or press Enter)

If you answer incorrectly, the message changes to: *"Your answer wasn't correct, but that's okay! Can you explain your reasoning? What made you choose that answer?"*

The Tutor helps without giving away the answer. It may:

- **Clarify** – Explain the concept you’re missing
- **Decompose** – Ask simpler sub-questions to guide you step by step

You can have several back-and-forth rounds until you understand.

### 2. Ask Tutor (One-Click Help)

For question banks like Surgery Domain Questions, an **Ask Tutor** button appears next to the Domain/Topic/Subtopic line.

- Click **Ask Tutor** to get help right away, without typing your reasoning first
- The Tutor will analyze the question and provide guidance

### 3. Free Chat

At any time, you can type a medical question or comment in the input box and click **Send**. The Tutor knows the current question and will answer with context and source citations when helpful.

---

## Your Progress

### Questions Answered

The app counts how many questions you’ve submitted. This is shown in the header area.

### Knowledge Coverage

**Knowledge Coverage** (e.g. 0% (0/193 subtopics)) shows what percentage of subtopics you’ve mastered based on your answers.

**By domain (accuracy %)** lists each knowledge domain (e.g. Abdominal Wall & Hernias, Critical Care) with your accuracy for that domain (e.g. 0% (0/24)).

- **Correct answers** – Help build your coverage
- **Wrong answers** – May lower coverage for that topic

Your progress is saved and carried over between sessions.

---

## Usage Limit

To keep the system fair for everyone, there is a usage limit for the Tutor. If you see a message like “Cost limit exceeded” with a countdown:

- Wait until the countdown finishes
- The Tutor will work again once the timer reaches zero

|------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|

| Submit an answer             | Click **Submit**                               |
| Get quick help (some banks)  | Click **Ask Tutor** next to the question       |
| Explain your reasoning       | Type in the right panel and click **Send**     |

## Need Help?

- **Can’t sign in?** Make sure your username and password are correct. If you created an account, sign in with those details.
- **Tutor not responding?** You may have hit the usage limit. Wait for the countdown to finish and try again.
- **Questions not loading?** Refresh the page. If it still doesn’t work, contact your administrator.
